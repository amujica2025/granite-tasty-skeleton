\# Watchlist Agent — Surface Vision



\## Surface Purpose

The Watchlist surface is the user’s market navigation layer.



It should answer:

\- Which watchlist group should I care about right now?

\- Which tickers inside that group deserve attention?

\- What is moving?

\- What does it cost to trade expected-move butterflies around this name?

\- Should I escalate this to Artifact?



\## Surface Structure

The Watchlist panel should feel like a hierarchical market tree.



\### Parent Level

Parent rows represent watchlists:

\- Weeklys

\- each SPDR sector watchlist



These parent rows should remain visible even when collapsed.



\### Child Level

Expanded child rows represent tradable tickers inside the selected watchlist.



These child rows should be information-dense and scan-friendly.



\## Chart Relationship

The lower 1/3 mini chart is not the main charting surface.

It exists to provide immediate visual context for the highlighted ticker.



Artifact remains the deep charting destination.



\## Trading Intent

The Watchlist is not only a navigation layer.

It is also a quick options-costing surface through its upper/lower expected-move fly columns.



This makes the Watchlist actionable, not just informational.
