\# Scanner Agent — Logic Principles



\## Core Principle 1

A scanner result is not useful unless it is comparable on the basis the user actually trades.



That means:

\- normalized risk

\- actual capital effect

\- same-underlying context where relevant



\## Core Principle 2

Rolls are not isolated spread replacements.



They are transformations of an existing book state.

Therefore roll logic must account for:

\- what is being changed

\- what is left behind

\- what the broker capital effect becomes after the change



\## Core Principle 3

Mini vol surface is not decoration.



Its job is to quickly show:

\- relative term structure

\- strike/expiration richness

\- how options are being priced now



\## Core Principle 4

Entry and Roll belong together because they are both capital-allocation decisions.
