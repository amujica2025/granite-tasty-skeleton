\# Scanner Agent — Surface Vision



\## Surface Purpose

The Scanner surface is the user’s decision engine.



It should answer:

\- Where should I deploy capital right now?

\- Which entry gives the best premium for the real risk/capital used?

\- What roll opportunities exist for what I already hold?

\- How are options being priced across nearby expirations?

\- Where is premium relatively rich or poor?



\## Surface Structure

Scanner is one panel with three conceptual regions:



\### Top region

Mode control and filters

\- Entry / Roll toggle

\- compact filters

\- ranking mode



\### Middle region

Dense results table

\- candidates

\- sortable

\- comparable

\- no card clutter



\### Lower region

Mini vol surface

\- selected underlying only

\- next 5–10 expirations

\- intuitive quick pricing context



\## Relationship to Other Surfaces

\- Watchlist may feed symbol selection into Scanner

\- Positions may feed current open-leg context into Roll mode

\- Artifact may later open for deeper study of a selected candidate



Scanner does not replace those surfaces.

It converts context into decisions.



\## User Goal

The user should feel:

“This panel helps me decide what to do next.”
